<h3>Diversity Statement</h3>
My experiences have greatly experienced who I am and my approach to research. Being a first-generation college student and getting my start in community college, I am dedicated towards increasing awareness, resources, and support for those discovering careers in academia. As a senior, just starting out in research, I heard from peers that you needed four years minimum to get into and succeed in a Ph.D. program. Having transferred to UC Berkeley as a junior, this seemed impossible without significant personal cost. Perceptions like this, as well as hidden curriculum and institutional knowledge, are things I actively work towards dismantling.
I believe anyone can succeed in this field with enough curiosity, persistence, passion for learning, and drive to generate new knowledge. To that end, I am serving on UW Psychology’s Inclusive Excellence committee, working on the staff belonging subcommittee (pushing projects for lab manager inclusion), and on the graduate student belonging subcommittee (supporting the collection, analysis, and presentation of the climate survey). At Stanford I chaired the Diversity Committee, where I led Paths to Ph.D., a webinar and workshop dedicated towards demystifying hidden Ph.D. application curriculum , particularly for those from minoritized backgrounds.
</p>
<p>
I am dedicated to being curious, not just about the topic we research, but people and their experiences. 
I aim to give everyone and their writing the most charitable interpretation. 
I strive to make knowledge as accessible as possible.
</p>
