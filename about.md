<h3 class="fw-bold border-bottom pb-3 mb-5">Diversity Statement</h3>

My experiences have greatly shaped who I am and my approach to research. Being a first-generation college student and getting my start in community college, I am dedicated to increasing awareness, resources, and support for those discovering careers in academia. As a senior just starting out in research, I heard from peers that you needed four years minimum to get into and succeed in a Ph.D. program. Having transferred to UC Berkeley as a junior, this seemed impossible without significant personal cost. Perceptions like this, as well as hidden curriculum and institutional knowledge, are things I actively work to dismantle.

I believe anyone can succeed in this field with enough curiosity, persistence, passion for learning, and drive to generate new knowledge. To that end, I serve on UW Psychology's Inclusive Excellence Committee, working on both the staff belonging subcommittee and the graduate student belonging subcommittee. At Stanford I chaired the Diversity Committee, where I led Paths to Ph.D., a webinar and workshop dedicated to demystifying the hidden curriculum of Ph.D. applications, particularly for students from historically underrepresented backgrounds.

I am dedicated to being curious—not just about the topics we research, but also about people and their experiences. I aim to give everyone and their writing the most charitable interpretation and strive to make knowledge as accessible as possible.
